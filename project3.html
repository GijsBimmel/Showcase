Redesign Bring Online

User-Centered Website Design

For my internship project, I was given the challenge of completely redesigning the Bring Online website. The company has been growing quickly and now offers many more services than before, but that growth wasn’t reflected on the existing website. Visitors often didn’t fully understand what Bring Online actually does, which meant missing out on potential clients. I wanted to create a new website that not only looked more modern and professional, but also clearly showed how Bring Online can help local entrepreneurs.

During the project, I discovered that the main issue was the way information was structured and presented. Important services weren’t highlighted properly, the layout was unclear, and the overall look no longer matched the current identity of Bring Online. That’s why I started researching how to improve the website so that visitors could immediately understand what the company offers and why they should choose Bring Online. This was essential for improving online visibility and attracting new clients.

From this, I defined the main goal: to develop a completely renewed, user-friendly, faster, and more professional website. A website that works well on all devices and clearly communicates the company’s services. The final product is a fully redesigned WordPress website with a fresh visual style, improved navigation, and clear, structured content that helps entrepreneurs quickly find the information they need and encourages them to get in touch.

To achieve this, I formulated several research questions that guided me through the design process. These questions helped me stay focused on what users needed, what wasn’t working on the old website, and how I could solve those issues. Using the Design Thinking method, I worked step by step, researching, testing, and designing, so that the final product truly fits the needs of local entrepreneurs and aligns with Bring Online’s identity.
